1. FUNDAMENTALS EVERY APPRENTICE MUST KNOW
	•	Keep panel doors closed.
	•	De-energize whenever possible.
	•	Voltage doesn’t lie — verify with your meter.
	•	Tighten terminations to spec.
	•	Never assume a neutral is safe.
	•	Bond everything metallic.
	•	Always pull a ground.

⸻

2. PANELS: SERVICE VS SUBPANELS

Service Panels:
	•	Neutral & ground bonded.
	•	First means of disconnect.

Subpanels:
	•	Floating neutral bar.
	•	Grounds isolated & bonded to enclosure.

Install Steps:
	1.	Mount level.
	2.	Bond/float correctly.
	3.	Land feeder in order: ground → neutral → hots.
	4.	Label everything.

3. BREAKERS

GFCI: Wet locations.
2-pole: 240V loads & MWBCs.

4. WIRE SIZING

6. CONDUIT BENDING

Stub Up Take-Ups:
	•	1/2” = 5”
	•	3/4” = 6”

Offset Formula:
Offset height ÷ multiplier.
Multipliers:
	•	10° = 5.7
	•	22° = 2.6
	•	30° = 2.0
	•	45° = 1.4

10. MULTIMETER & CLAMP METER ULTIMATE GUIDE

Measurements:

Voltage AC: Testing panels, breakers, motors, transformers.
Voltage DC: Batteries, solar, controls.
Continuity: Verifying closed switches, coils, fuses.
Resistance: Transformers, coils, heaters.
Current (Clamp): Motor inrush, breaker loading.

How To Test Anything:

Transformers:
	1.	Check primary voltage.
	2.	Check secondary voltage.
	3.	Ohm primary/secondary open.

Contactors/Relays:
	•	Coil ohms OK?
	•	Coil getting power?
	•	Contacts open/closed correctly?

Motors:
	•	Voltage correct?
	•	Amps = 80–100% FLA.
	•	Meg if required.

11. TROUBLESHOOTING

If nothing works:
	1.	Check main.
	2.	Check panel breaker.
	3.	Check load.
	4.	Check switch/relay.

Motor won’t start:
	•	Control circuit open.
	•	Overload tripped.
	•	Coil not powered.

Breaker trips:
	•	Dead short.
	•	Undersized wire.
	•	Motor locked.

ELECTRICIAN’S FIELD SURVIVAL GUIDE
Fold it, stain it, laminate it. Keep it in your tool pouch until it’s unreadable.
----
1. FIRST-DAY RULES (TATTOO THESE)
1.  If it’s live, you’re gambling. De-energize, LOTO, test, touch.
2.  If you don’t know, ask. 2 minutes of “dumb” questions beats 2 weeks in a cast.
3.  Your knife is for wire, not fingers. Cut away from body, always.
4.  If the journeyman says “Hurry up,” you still slow down. Speed comes from smooth, not sloppy.
5.  Take a photo before you disconnect anything. Phone eats first, every time.
----
2. TOOL POUCH MUST-HAVOS (DON’T SHOW UP WITHOUT)
•  11-in-1 – lives in your hand, not the pouch.
•  Stripper-crimper combo – #10–#14 slots only.
•  Tick tester – check it on a known hot first, every morning.
•  Mini level – panels that look crooked = instant “rookie” label.
•  Sharpie & white tape – label while you work, not after.
•  Headlamp – because panels are always in dark corners.
•  Small torque screwdriver – 12–20 in-lb stops callbacks.
----
3. PANELS: 30-SECOND CHECKLIST
1.  Door closed? Open it like it owes you money – one hand only, face turned.
2.  Bonding screw in or out? Service panel = IN, sub-panel = OUT.
3.  Neutrals & grounds on separate bars in subs – if they touch, you just created a parallel path and future ghost trips.
4.  Feeder lands: ground → neutral → hots – keeps your wrench away from live lugs.
5.  Label BEFORE you land wires – you’ll forget once it’s spaghetti.
----
4. WIRE SIZING & BREAKERS (MEMORY TRICK)
“14-50-65-100”
•  #14 = 15 A
•  #12 = 20 A
•  #10 = 30 A
•  #8 = 50 A
•  #6 = 65 A
•  #4 = 85 A
•  #3 = 100 A
Breaker size ≤ wire ampacity.
Voltage drop? If run > 100 ft, go one size bigger – cheaper than a callback.
----
5. CONDUIT FILL – “FIST RULE”
•  ½” EMT = closed fist = 6–7 #12 THHN.
•  ¾” EMT = two-finger peace sign = 16 #12.
•  1” EMT = OK sign = 26 #12.
Overfill = pull burns, failed inspection, and your foreman’s death stare.
----
6. BENDING 101 (NO PROTRACTOR NEEDED)
•  Stub-up: mark length, subtract 5” for ½”, 6” for ¾”, bend 90.
•  Offset:
•  22° = bend in the air, kick looks neat, multiplier 2.6.
•  30° = fast and dirty, multiplier 2.0.
Measure center-to-center of conduit, not edge.
----
7. DEVICE WIRING – COLOR INSIDE HEAD
•  Brass = hot = black (or red)
•  Silver = neutral = white
•  Green = ground = bare or green
Wrap terminals clockwise so screw tightens, not pushes, the wire.
Pigtail neutrals – never rely on device to carry neutral downstream.
----
8. MOTOR QUICK-TEST
1.  Voltage at T-leads? Yes → step 2.
2.  Amps on all three legs? Within 10% of each other.
3.  OL reset pushed? If it clicks, find the bind or overload before restart.
If it hums and smokes, kill it. Megger if you have one, swap if you don’t.
----
9. TROUBLESHOOTING IN 4 MOVES
1.  Breaker on? Test both screws.
2.  Neutral landed? Wiggle test.
3.  Device good? Swap with known good.
4.  Still dead? Follow the homerun – open every box upstream until you find power.
Work backwards from load to panel – faster than guessing.
----
10. PRINTS & MARK-UPS
•  Red pen = what you moved.
•  Blue highlighter = circuits you powered up.
•  Green pencil = future stuff – don’t energize yet.
Snap a pic of every redline – that’s your as-built when the architect “loses” the set.
----
11. COMMON JOBSITE FAILS (DON’T BE THAT GUY)
•  Forgot ring on MC – inspector sees it, you pull the whole run.
•  Drywall guys bury your boxes – take 30 sec to snap a photo & flag.
•  Landing #10 on 20 A breaker – instant fail.
•  Using duct tape instead of 2000 tape on PVC – it slips, glue it right.
•  Leaving wire nuts in panel – rookie billboard.
----
12. MAGIC PHRASES THAT SAVE YOUR BUTT
•  “I need to verify that with the foreman.”
•  “Can you show me on the print?”
•  “I’m not comfortable working it hot.”
•  “Let me LOTO that first.”
Say them out loud. Nobody ever got fired for being too safe.
----
13. DAILY 2-MINUTE DRILL (BEFORE YOU BOUNCE)
1.  All screws tight? Give every device a quick tug.
2.  Panels closed, breakers labeled?
3.  Trash & wire scraps off the floor – GC notices.
4.  Photos of anything weird – send to foreman now, not Monday.
5.  Tools accounted for? Replacing them beats explaining why they’re in the ceiling.
----

“Plan like it’s rocket science, work like you’re lazy, test like you’re paranoid.”

3. SERVICE TYPES & HOW TO LAND THEM
A. OVERHEAD (TRI-plex or Quad-plex)
•  House 100-200 A: 2 hots (aluminum AA-1350) twisted around bare neutral messenger.
•  Landing: Top lugs of meter base → SE-U into main breaker.
•  Torque: 35 in-lb #2 Al, 50 in-lb 4/0 Al.
•  Rule: Drip-loop 12” below weather-head, neutral taped white in panel.
B. UNDERGROUND (URD in conduit)
•  200 A: 4/0 Al XHHW-2, 2” PVC.
•  400 A: parallel 4/0 or 250 kcmil, 3” PVC.
•  Landing: Same as overhead, but add expansion joint every 100 ft (slab crossing).
•  Warning: Back-fill 6” sand, warning tape 12” above – inspector pulls it up.
C. COMMERCIAL 400-1 200 A (CT Cabinet)
•  Line side: Utility seals CT cabinet – you land on line-side lugs only.
•  Load side: Your feeders hit main lugs in switchboard – no breaker ahead of CT.
•  Grounding: 400 A = #3/0 Cu GEC to water & ground rod.
•  Fire seal: 3M putty in every 3” conduit entry – AHJ checks.
D. 3-PHASE DELTA (240 V)
•  High-leg = orange wire (B-phase) – lands on RIGHT lug of main breaker.
•  Panel schedule: Mark “High-Leg 208 V – DO NOT USE FOR 120 V”.
•  Overload: Size breakers at 125% of largest motor + 100% of rest – NEC 430.52.
E. 3-PHASE WYE (480 V)
•  Sequence: Brown–Orange–Yellow (BOY) – A-B-C top-down.
•  Neutral: Same lug bar, but size at 100% of largest unbalance – usually full-size.
•  GFPD: 277 V lighting = nuisance trip city – use 30 mA GFP breakers, not 5 mA.

6. TYPICAL HOOK-UP SEQUENCE (ANY PANEL)
1.  Kill everything – LOTO, verify zero energy.
2.  Mount panel – 30” wide × 36” deep × 6’-7” max breaker height.
3.  Install ground bar kit (sub-panel) – do NOT bond.
4.  Feeder into top – ground first, neutral second, hots last.
5.  Torque ALL lugs – stamp the panel label with torque value; inspector loves it.
6.  Label directory – pencil first, permanent marker after trim-out.
7.  Take photo – future-you will thank present-you.

7. SPECIAL SCENARIOS
Shed / Detached Garage
•  4-wire SER or THWN in PVC – bury 18” below grade (no GFCI protection on feeder).
•  Ground rod(s): 5/8 × 8’ driven flush, clamp #6 Cu. Resistance ≤ 25 Ω – if not, second rod 6 ft away.
•  Panel: smallest 60 A main-lug kit with 8 spaces – cheaper than 4-space + feed-through lugs.
•  Bonding screw removed, neutral bar floats.
Mobile Home
•  Service rated at 200 A outdoor panel – meter-main combo.
•  Feeder to home: 4-wire mobile-home feeder (MHF) – same sizes as SER but sunlight-resistant.
•  Ground neutral inside combo – inside home panel they float.
Office Suite (Tenant)
•  Landlord provides 480 V panel – you get 208 V/120 V step-down transformer + 42-circuit panel.
•  Transformer secondary: size neutral 100% – harmonic loads (computers).
•  Panel schedule: add “Panel A-1” sticker on door – fire marshal checks during annual.
Rooftop HVAC / RTU
•  480 V 3-ph 30 A – run #10 THHN in ¾” EMT, individual EGC (#10 green).
•  Local disco within sight – 30 A non-fused safety switch, lockable.
•  Panel breaker: HMCP 40 A with 125% FLA setting – starts easier, no nuisance trip.
