## 🧠 The “Learn This to Sound Smart” Short List

You don’t need to memorize the NEC. Just get comfortable with these **five concepts**. If you can casually mention them in an interview, you’ll sound like a first‑year who’s already ahead.

### 1. **Safety – Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)**
> “I always verify the circuit is dead with a meter before touching anything, and I understand the importance of locking out breakers so no one accidentally energizes it.”

### 2. **Wire Sizes & Ampacity**
> “I know that #14 copper is rated for 15 amps, #12 for 20 amps, and #10 for 30 amps. I’m still learning how temperature and wire type affect that, but I understand the basic ampacity table.”

- Memorize this **simple chart**:

| Wire Size (Copper) | Ampacity (general) |
| ------------------ | ------------------ |
| #14 AWG            | 15 A               |
| #12 AWG            | 20 A               |
| #10 AWG            | 30 A               |
| #8 AWG             | 40–50 A (depends)  |

### 3. **Box Fill – Why It Matters**
> “I know that boxes have a maximum number of wires based on their cubic inch capacity. I’m used to counting conductors from my low‑voltage work, and I understand that in high voltage you also have to count devices and clamps.”

- **Simple rule:** A standard plastic nail‑on box for a switch or receptacle is usually sized for a certain number of #12 wires. If you add too many, you fail inspection.

### 4. **GFCI vs. AFCI**
> “I know GFCI protects people from shock near water (kitchens, baths, outdoors). AFCI protects against fire by detecting arcing in bedrooms and living areas. Both are required by code now.”

### 5. **Conduit Fill & Bending Basics**
> “I’ve pulled wire through conduit in low voltage. I understand that conduit can only be filled to 40% for more than 2 wires (per NEC Chapter 9), and I’m eager to learn how to bend EMT with a hand bender.”

# Part 2: SkillCat / Trade Knowledge Refresher
In an interview, they want to know if you can think like an electrician, not just read a book. Here is the practical "Apprentice Level" knowledge.

### 1. The Theory (Ohm’s Law)
You must be able to draw and explain this on a whiteboard if asked.
- **Voltage (E):** The pressure (Volts).
- **Current (I):** The flow (Amps).
- **Resistance (R):** The opposition (Ohms).
- **Formula:** \( E = I \times R \)
- **Power:** \( P = E \times I \) (Watts)

### 2. The Three Types of Loads
- **Resistive:** Heat, incandescent lights. (Easy on breakers).
- **Inductive:** Motors, fans. (Has inrush current; requires larger breakers sometimes).
- **Capacitive:** Power supplies, VFDs. (Can cause harmonic issues).

### 3. Conduit Bending (The Interview Question)
If they ask about bending, they are testing if you’ve been in the field.
- **Stub-up (90°):** Shrink is about \( \frac{1}{2} \) the depth of the bend. Deduct (take-up) varies by bender (e.g., 6" for \( \frac{1}{2} \)" EMT).
- **Offset:** Used to go around an obstacle. The angle determines the multiplier: 10° (x 5.8), 30° (x 2.0), 45° (x 1.4).
- **Box Offset (3-point saddle):** Used to go over a pipe or small obstruction.

### 4. Common Mistakes to Avoid (Interview Red Flags)
If you are interviewing, admit you don't know something, but show you know how to find it. Never say:
- *"I just use whatever wire is on the truck."* (Wrong: You must size by ampacity).
- *"Grounding and bonding are the same thing."* (Wrong: Grounding is a path to earth; Bonding is a path to trip the breaker).
- *"I never use a torque screwdriver."* (Wrong: NEC 110.14 requires torqueing lugs).

# Part 3: The Daily Study Plan (7 Days to Pro)

To become "pro," you need repetition. Spend **1 hour** per day on this rotation.

### Day 1: Safety & Basic Theory
- **Study:** OSHA Focus Four (Electrical, Falls, Caught-In, Struck-By). Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures.
- **Code:** Article 100 (Definitions). Know the difference between a *Dwelling Unit*, *Outlet*, and *Receptacle*.
- **SkillCat Task:** Practice Ohm’s Law calculations.

### Day 2: Box Fill & Conduit Fill
- **Study:** NEC 314.16 (Box Fill). Memorize: Conductor count = volume allowance. Don't forget internal clamps and devices count.
- **SkillCat Task:** Calculate how many #12 wires fit in a 4x1.5 square box (Answer: \( 3 \times 2.25 \) = 6.75? Actually, look up the table. It’s about 9 #12s).
- **NEC Focus:** Annex C (Conduit fill tables) – learn how to look up max conductors.

### Day 3: Residential (The 6-12 Rule)
- **Study:** Article 210.52 (Receptacle placement).
    - **Wall:** No point > 6 ft from a receptacle. (Thus, 12 ft spacing max).
    - **Countertop:** Receptacle above counter within 2 ft of the edge. No point > 24 inches without a receptacle.
- **Study:** **AFCI (210.12)** vs **GFCI (210.8)** .
    - *GFCI:* Near water (kitchen, bath, outdoors, garage).
    - *AFCI:* Living areas (bedrooms, living room, etc.) to detect arcing.

### Day 4: Commercial / Industrial
- **Study:** **310.16 (Ampacity)** .
    - Memorize: #14 = 15A, #12 = 20A, #10 = 30A (Copper, 60°C/75°C).
- **Study:** **250.122 (Equipment Grounding Conductor)** .
    - Memorize: #12 circuit = #12 ground; #10 circuit = #10 ground; #8 circuit = #10 ground.
- **Study:** Conduit types: EMT (thinwall), RMC (rigid), PVC, and where you can use them (EMT indoors, PVC in corrosive areas).

### Day 5: Motors & Specialty
- **Study:** Article 430. Motors are a beast, but as a 2nd year, know that motor breakers can be larger than wire (to handle starting current) and that you need overload protection at the motor.
- **SkillCat Task:** Learn to read a motor nameplate (FLA - Full Load Amps).

### Day 6: Grounding & Bonding (The Deep Dive)
- **Study:** **Article 250 Part III (Grounding Electrode System)** .
    - What is a grounding electrode? (Ground rod, metal water pipe, building steel).
    - **250.56:** One ground rod is not enough unless you prove < 25 ohms; otherwise, drive two rods 6 ft apart.
- **Study:** **Main Bonding Jumper** (at the service panel) vs **Equipment Grounding Conductor** (in the branch circuit).

### Day 7: The "Mock Interview" & NEC Navigation
- **SkillCat Task:** Time yourself.
    - *Scenario:* I am installing a pool pump. Where do I go? (Answer: Article 680).
    - *Scenario:* I need to support MC cable. Where do I go? (Answer: 330.30).
- **The Interview Script:** Practice saying: *"If I don't know the exact Code reference, I know how to use the index and the thumb tabs to find the answer within 60 seconds."*

# Part 4: Quick Reference Cheat Sheet (Print This)

### Wire Sizing (Copper THHN/THWN 75°C)
| Conductor   | Ampacity | Typical Use                                                   |
| :---------- | :------- | :------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **#14 AWG** | 15 Amps  | Lighting, general receptacles (rarely used in commercial now) |
| **#12 AWG** | 20 Amps  | Standard receptacle circuits, kitchens                        |
| **#10 AWG** | 30 Amps  | Water heaters, A/C units, dryers (small)                      |
| **#8 AWG**  | 50 Amps  | Ranges, small subpanels                                       |
| **#6 AWG**  | 65 Amps  | Large subpanels, EV chargers                                  |

### Working Space (110.26)
- **0–150 Volts:** 3 ft clearance (depth).
- **151–600 Volts:** 4 ft clearance.
- **Height:** 6.5 ft headroom.

### Box Fill (314.16)
- **#14 wire:** 2.00 cubic inches per conductor.
- **#12 wire:** 2.25 cubic inches per conductor.
- **#10 wire:** 2.50 cubic inches per conductor.
- *Count:* All grounds = 1 count; All internal clamps = 1 count; Each device (receptacle/switch) = 2 counts.

### Key Acronyms
- **NEC:** National Electrical Code (NFPA 70)
- **GFCI:** Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (protects people)
- **AFCI:** Arc Fault Circuit Interrupter (protects the structure)
- **EGC:** Equipment Grounding Conductor (green wire)
- **GEC:** Grounding Electrode Conductor (goes to rod/water pipe)
- **MC:** Metal Clad cable
- **EMT:** Electrical Metallic Tubing
