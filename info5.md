1. WHAT ELECTRICITY ACTUALLY IS
Electricity is the movement of electrons through a conductor.
Copper wire contains free electrons.
Voltage pushes electrons.
Current is the movement of electrons.
Resistance slows electron flow.
Power is the work electricity performs.

2. THE 4 CORE ELECTRICAL VALUES
Symbol	Meaning	Unit
V	Voltage	Volts
I	Current	Amps
R	Resistance	Ohms
P	Power	Watts
3. SIMPLE ELECTRIC THEORY
VOLTAGE
Electrical pressure.
Think:Water pressure in pipe.
Higher voltage = stronger push.
Examples:
* 120V receptacle
* 240V dryer
* 480V motor

CURRENT (AMPERAGE)
Amount of electrical flow.
Think:Amount of water flowing.
Too much current overheats wire.

RESISTANCE
Opposition to current flow.
Think:Small pipe restricting water.
Resistance creates:
* Heat
* Voltage drop
* Power loss

POWER
Electrical work being done.
Examples:
* Heat
* Motion
* Light
* Motor rotation

5. POWER FORMULAS
Basic Power Formula
P=VI

Find Amps
I=\frac{P}{V}

Find Voltage
V=\frac{P}{I}

6. AC vs DC
DC (Direct Current)
Flows one direction.
Examples:
* Batteries
* Solar
* Electronics

AC (Alternating Current)
Changes direction repeatedly.
US frequency:60Hz
Used in:
* Homes
* Commercial
* Industrial
Reason:Easy voltage transformation.

7. SINGLE PHASE POWER
Residential power.
Usually:120/240V
Transformer has center tap neutral.

Measurements
Hot → Neutral = 120V
Hot → Hot = 240V
Neutral → Ground ≈ 0V

Residential Conductors

12. GROUNDING vs BONDING
Grounding
Connection to earth.
Purpose:
* Stabilize voltage
* Lightning protection
* Surge path

Bonding
Connecting metal parts together.
Purpose:Create low-resistance fault path.
Trips breaker quickly.

13. MAIN PANEL THEORY
Main service disconnect:Neutral and ground bonded ONLY here.
Subpanels:Neutral and ground separated.
Huge inspection issue.

15. CONTINUOUS LOAD RULE
Continuous load:Runs 3+ hours.
Must size at 125%.

Example
20A circuit continuous load:
20A\times0.8=16A
Max continuous:16A

16. VOLTAGE DROP THEORY
Longer wire =More resistance =Voltage loss.

Target
Branch circuit:Under 3%
Entire feeder:Under 5%

17. VOLTAGE DROP FORMULA
VD=\frac{2KID}{CM}
Where:
* K = resistivity constant
* I = current
* D = distance
* CM = circular mil area
